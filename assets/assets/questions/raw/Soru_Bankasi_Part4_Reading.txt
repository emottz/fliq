--- METİN 2: WET MOTORING ---
"Wet motoring the engine is necessary to test the fuel system. When an engine is wet motored, the two ignition systems are turned OFF and the starter is engaged to turn the HP rotor to 15%-20% N2. At N2 = 15%, the HP fuel shutoff valve control lever is moved to ON and the engine exhaust nozzle is carefully monitored for any sign of fuel. The test can be continued in accordance with starter duty cycle limitations."

"Avionics equipment generates heat during operation and must be kept within operating temperature limits to function correctly. The avionics cooling system uses forced air to cool the electronic equipment. On the ground, the cooling fans must operate whenever the avionics are powered. In flight, ram air also assists in cooling. If the cooling system fails, the avionics equipment may overheat and malfunction. Most aircraft have temperature sensors on critical avionics boxes that trigger warnings if cooling is inadequate. Regular inspection of the cooling fans, ducts, and filters is required as part of the maintenance program."

--- METİN 21: NON-DESTRUCTIVE TESTING ---
"Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) is used to inspect aircraft structures and components without causing damage. Common NDT methods used in aviation include visual inspection, eddy current testing, ultrasonic testing, radiographic testing, and magnetic particle inspection. Eddy current testing is particularly effective for detecting surface and sub-surface cracks in conductive materials. Ultrasonic testing uses sound waves to detect internal flaws in materials. Radiographic testing uses X-rays to reveal internal defects. All NDT methods require properly certified inspectors and calibrated equipment to produce reliable results."

--- METİN 22: FUEL SYSTEM COMPONENTS ---

--- METİN 23: FLIGHT CONTROL SYSTEMS ---
"The flight control system allows the pilots to maneuver the aircraft in all three axes. Primary flight controls include ailerons (roll), elevators (pitch), and rudder (yaw). Maintenance personnel must never operate the flight controls when technicians are working on them unless a clear all-clear signal has been given."

--- METİN 24: AIRCRAFT TYRE MAINTENANCE ---
"Aircraft tyres are critical components that support the full weight of the aircraft on the ground. Tyres must be maintained at the correct inflation pressure. Under-inflation increases tyre wear and generates excessive heat during high-speed ground operations. Over-inflation reduces the tyre contact area and can cause premature failure. Tyre pressure must be checked with the aircraft unloaded (jacked up) and with the tyre at ambient temperature, as the pressure changes with temperature and under load. Tyres must be inspected for cuts, bulges, and excessive wear before each flight. When wear indicators reach their limit, the tyre must be replaced."

--- METİN 25: BATTERY MAINTENANCE ---
"Aircraft batteries are used for engine starting and as a backup power source during electrical emergencies. Lead-acid and nickel-cadmium (NiCd) batteries are commonly used. Lead-acid batteries can produce explosive mixtures of hydrogen and oxygen during charging and discharging, which can ignite if a spark is present. Batteries must be checked regularly for charge capacity, electrolyte level, and condition of the terminals. If the battery remains connected to the aircraft for extended periods without charge, deep discharge can occur and permanently damage the battery. Batteries must be stored in well-ventilated areas away from heat sources."

--- METİN 27: SAFETY PRECAUTIONS IN MAINTENANCE ---

--- METİN 28: ENGINE TURBINE SECTION ---
"The turbine section of a gas turbine engine extracts energy from the hot, high-pressure gas stream produced by the combustion process. The turbine blades rotate at extremely high speeds and temperatures, making them one of the most highly stressed components in the engine. High-pressure (HP) turbine stages are the first to receive the hot gases from the combustion chamber. Low-pressure (LP) turbine stages follow and extract more energy from the gas. Regular borescope inspections of the turbine section are required to detect cracks, erosion, and tip rubs. Turbine blades have a finite life limit measured in flight cycles."

--- METİN 29: COMPOSITE STRUCTURE REPAIR ---
"Composite materials are increasingly used in modern aircraft structures due to their high strength-to-weight ratio.
